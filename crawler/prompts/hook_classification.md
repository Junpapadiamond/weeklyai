# WeeklyAI Hook Classification v2

Use exactly one hook per product. Pick the strongest reason someone scrolling AI Twitter would stop and click.

## Priority When Multiple Hooks Match

1. `weird_form` wins when the physical product shape is the surprise.
2. `new_behavior` wins when AI can now do something materially different, especially from a famous brand.
3. `unexpected_combo` wins when the novelty is connecting AI to an unlikely object, domain, or workflow.
4. `new_interaction` wins when the input/output mode is the surprise.
5. `quiet_real_problem` wins when the product is not flashy but clearly solves a painful, real task.
6. `niche_depth` wins when the product is narrow but unusually complete for its niche.

## weird_form

Color: `#5d8a4e`

Definition: AI is packaged into a non-traditional physical form that makes the product feel immediately inspectable.

Positive examples:
- AI 盆栽: a plant-like device that reminds you to water it by voice; the form is the hook.
- Plaud NotePin: a wearable pin for meeting memory; it turns transcription into a small object.
- Friend pendant: an AI companion as jewelry; the physical carrier changes the emotional read.

Counterexamples:
- Another AI smart speaker; traditional form, even if the model is new.
- A normal smartwatch with an AI assistant; expected carrier.
- A mobile app that uses plant imagery; visual metaphor is not physical form.

## new_behavior

Color: `#d85a30`

Definition: AI can perform a new class of action or operate with a materially longer, more autonomous, or more capable behavior.

Positive examples:
- Operator-style browser or terminal agents that complete multi-step tasks without hand-holding.
- A coding agent that can run tests, edit files, and recover from errors for extended work loops.
- A robot or device that initiates context-aware action instead of only responding to commands.

Counterexamples:
- Model version bumps with no new user-visible behavior.
- Lower pricing, more languages, bigger context windows without a new behavior.
- A known chatbot adding a normal sidebar or UI refresh.

## unexpected_combo

Color: `#b94c87`

Definition: AI is attached to an object, audience, or workflow where people do not expect it, and the combination creates a real product idea.

Positive examples:
- AI pet collar that interprets behavior and gives care suggestions.
- AI bathroom mirror that turns routines into health coaching.
- AI NAS that classifies home media and suggests automations locally.

Counterexamples:
- Generic AI CRM/email/document tools; expected categories.
- A normal IoT device with a chatbot bolted on but no changed workflow.
- A novelty object that only plays prerecorded lines.

## quiet_real_problem

Color: `#5a6c7d`

Definition: The product solves a specific painful problem in a surprisingly direct way, even if it is visually plain.

Positive examples:
- Legal redaction agent that reliably removes sensitive data before sharing.
- Medical record summarizer that turns messy visits into structured patient handoff notes.
- Compliance assistant that watches a workflow and creates an audit trail automatically.

Counterexamples:
- Generic productivity copilots with broad claims.
- Dashboards that only summarize existing metrics.
- Enterprise AI announcements with no concrete workflow improvement.

## new_interaction

Color: `#b8a13a`

Definition: The product changes how people communicate with AI through sensing, gesture, ambient context, voice, vision, or spatial interaction.

Positive examples:
- A lamp that responds to gestures and room context instead of chat text.
- AR glasses where the main interface is gaze plus voice over the real world.
- A meeting device that tags moments by tapping or squeezing, not opening an app.

Counterexamples:
- A chatbot with a nicer prompt box.
- Voice input added to an otherwise normal assistant.
- A workflow automation that is powerful but uses standard forms and buttons.

## niche_depth

Color: `#6d4ea5`

Definition: The product is narrow, but goes unusually deep on one audience, object, or job.

Positive examples:
- AI assistant only for aquarium water quality that reads tests, fish behavior, and equipment.
- AI copilot for patent claim charts with domain-specific evidence mapping.
- Model-powered tool for one obscure manufacturing inspection step.

Counterexamples:
- Broad "AI for teams" products.
- Thin wrappers around generic LLM prompts.
- A niche landing page with no specialized behavior.
